Definition:
One-Hot Encoding is the simplest text vectorization technique.
Each word in the vocabulary is represented by a unique binary vector.
Value 1 → word is present
Value 0 → word is absent
Vector length = size of vocabulary

1️ . Bag of Words (BoW)
######## Definition

Bag of Words is a technique where:

We ignore grammar and word order

We only count how many times each word appears

It creates a vocabulary of all unique words in the dataset.
Example

Sentence 1:

"I love AI"

Sentence 2:

"I love coding"

Step 1: Create Vocabulary

[I, love, AI, coding]

Step 2: Convert to Vectors
Sentence	I	love	AI	coding
I love AI	1	1	1	0
I love coding	1	1	0	1

Each sentence becomes a numeric vector.

@@@@@ Advantages

Simple to implement

Works well for small tasks

Easy to understand

@@@@@ Disadvantages

Ignores word order

No semantic meaning

Large vocabulary → large vectors (sparse matrix problem)

2️....N-gram
Definition

An N-gram is a sequence of N consecutive words.

1 word → Unigram

2 words → Bigram

3 words → Trigram

Example

Sentence:

"I love machine learning"

Unigrams (N=1)

I, love, machine, learning

Bigrams (N=2)

I love, love machine, machine learning

Trigrams (N=3)

I love machine, love machine learning

1️⃣ Term Frequency (TF)
👉 Definition:

Term Frequency measures how frequently a word appears in a single document.

👉 Formula:

TF(t,d)=
Total number of words in document d / Number of times term t appears in document d
	​

👉 Example:
tf - idf = 'ai is the future of ai';

2️⃣ Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

👉 Problem with TF:
Some words appear frequently in almost every document (like "is", "the", "and").
These words are not useful for distinguishing documents.
That’s where IDF comes in.

👉 Definition:
Inverse Document Frequency measures how important a word is across all documents.
If a word appears in many documents → less important
If it appears in very few documents → more important



👉 Formula:
IDF(t)=log⁡(Ndf(t))IDF(t) = \log \left( \frac{N}{df(t)} \right)IDF(t)=log(df(t)N​)
Where:


N = Total number of documents


df(t) = Number of documents containing term t



👉 Example:
"ai is the future"
"ai and ml are related"
"deep seek is powerful"
Suppose:


Total documents (N) = 1000


Word "AI" appears in 10 documents


IDF(AI)=log⁡(1000/10)=log⁡(100)=2IDF(AI) = \log(1000/10) = \log(100) = 2IDF(AI)=log(1000/10)=log(100)=2
If a word appears in 900 documents:
IDF=log⁡(1000/900)IDF = \log(1000/900)IDF=log(1000/900)
Very small value → less important

3️⃣ TF-IDF (Combined)
                       TF−IDF=TF×IDF
So a word is important if:

It appears many times in the document (high TF)

It appears in few documents overall (high IDF)
